# GL.iNet Router Comparison Guide (2026)

GL.iNet's lineup has gotten much more interesting over the last year. It is no longer just a choice between a small travel router and a bigger home router. Today, GL.iNet has strong options across **home**, **travel**, and **cellular** categories, including several **Wi-Fi 7** models and multiple **5G routers** built for very different use cases.

This guide compares the most popular current GL.iNet models and keeps the focus on what buyers usually care about most:

- **Where the router will be used**
- **How much VPN speed matters**
- **Whether portability matters more than raw performance**
- **Whether you need built-in 5G, a battery, or external antennas**

## Home Routers

### Flint 3 (GL-BE9300) — Best Overall Performance

If you want the strongest current GL.iNet home router, this is the one most people should start with. The Flint 3 is a **tri-band Wi-Fi 7** router with **five 2.5GbE ports**, making it especially attractive for multi-gig internet, heavier local traffic, faster backhaul, and users who want plenty of headroom.

**Why it stands out**
- Tri-band Wi-Fi 7
- Five 2.5GbE ports
- Strong WireGuard and OpenVPN-DCO performance
- Good fit for high-end home networks, lab setups, and small offices

**Best for**
- Multi-gig internet
- Power users
- Heavy VPN users who still want top-tier local networking

**Who should skip it**
- Buyers who want the lowest cost possible
- Users who do not need Wi-Fi 7 yet

---

### Flint 3e (GL-BE6500) — The Smarter Midpoint

The Flint 3e is one of the easiest routers in the lineup to recommend. It gives you **Wi-Fi 7** and **five 2.5GbE ports**, but at a more approachable tier than the Flint 3. For many buyers, it is the practical sweet spot between price and next-generation features.

**Why it stands out**
- Dual-band Wi-Fi 7
- Five 2.5GbE ports
- Similar listed VPN performance class to the Flint 3
- Great option for buyers who want Wi-Fi 7 without going all the way up to the flagship

**Best for**
- Buyers upgrading to Wi-Fi 7 on purpose
- Homes with multi-gig wired devices
- Users who want a newer platform without paying for tri-band

**Who should skip it**
- Users who specifically want tri-band Wi-Fi 7
- Budget-focused buyers who are happy with Wi-Fi 6

---

### Flint 2 (GL-MT6000) — Best Value Home Router

The Flint 2 remains one of GL.iNet's best overall routers. It is older than the Wi-Fi 7 models, but it is still a favorite because it combines **excellent VPN speed**, **Wi-Fi 6 stability**, and a very mature reputation in the lineup.

**Why it stands out**
- Wi-Fi 6 AX6000
- Up to 900 Mbps WireGuard
- Great for heavy home use, gaming, and advanced features
- Often the best value for users who care more about performance than having the newest Wi-Fi standard

**Best for**
- Home users who want proven performance
- VPN-heavy setups
- Buyers who prefer maturity over early-adopter hardware

**Who should skip it**
- Users determined to buy Wi-Fi 7
- Buyers who want the newest platform for longer-term future-proofing

## Travel Routers

### Slate 7 (GL-BE3600) — Best Premium Travel Router

The Slate 7 is GL.iNet's first dual-band Wi-Fi 7 travel router, and it feels like a major step up from earlier portable models. It combines modern wireless, **dual 2.5G ports**, and a **touchscreen**, which helps separate it from more traditional travel routers.

**Why it stands out**
- Dual-band Wi-Fi 7
- Dual 2.5GbE ports
- Touchscreen interface
- Strong fit for travelers who want a premium experience and faster wired links

**Best for**
- Frequent travelers
- Hotel and remote-work setups
- Users who want the newest travel platform

**Who should skip it**
- Buyers who mostly care about maximum VPN speed per dollar
- Users who want the cheapest travel option

---

### Beryl 7 (GL-MT3600BE) — Best Travel Router for VPN Speed

If VPN speed is the main goal, the Beryl 7 is one of the most compelling routers GL.iNet currently sells. It is positioned as the fastest VPN router in the company's travel lineup and is a very strong pick for secure remote work, tunneling, and portable high-performance setups.

**Why it stands out**
- Dual-band Wi-Fi 7
- Up to 1,100 Mbps WireGuard
- Compact travel-oriented form factor
- Excellent pick when VPN throughput is the top priority

**Best for**
- VPN-first travel use
- Technical users
- Travelers who care more about throughput than touchscreen extras

**Who should skip it**
- Buyers who want the simplest UI-first experience
- Users who do not need high VPN throughput

### Beryl AX (GL-MT3000) — Best Budget Travel Router

The Beryl AX is still one of GL.iNet's most useful products because it hits a very practical balance of portability, price, and capability. It remains one of the easiest recommendations for people who want a compact travel router without stepping up to the newer Wi-Fi 7 models.

**Why it stands out**

- 2.5G port

- Great value

**Best for**

The Slate AX is not the newest model in the travel lineup, but it still makes sense for buyers who want something well-known, proven, and capable. It is a dependable choice when you care more about a familiar platform than maximum specs.

**Best for**

## Cellular Routers

### Mudi 7 (GL-E5800) — Best Premium Cellular Router

The Mudi 7 is the most ambitious cellular model in the current GL.iNet lineup. It combines **5G**, **tri-band Wi-Fi 7**, a **battery**, **2.5G networking**, and a more premium portable design. If you want the most feature-rich cellular GL.iNet device right now, this is it.

**Why it stands out**
- 5G NR
- Tri-band Wi-Fi 7
- Built-in battery
- 2.5G ports
- Premium mobile-first design

**Best for**
- Users who want the most advanced cellular model
- Travel plus mobile office setups
- Buyers who want a flagship all-in-one unit

**Who should skip it**
- Buyers who want a lower-cost cellular option
- Users who do not need Wi-Fi 7 in a mobile device

---

### Puli AX (GL-XE3000) — Best Portable 5G Router

The Puli AX is still one of the most practical cellular routers in the lineup because it combines **5G**, **Wi-Fi 6**, **dual-SIM**, and a **built-in 6400mAh battery**. That makes it a very strong option for field work, temporary installs, travel, and situations where dependable power is not guaranteed.

**Why it stands out**
- 5G NR
- Wi-Fi 6 AX3000
- Dual-SIM
- Built-in 6400mAh battery
- Up to 300 Mbps WireGuard

**Best for**
- Portable 5G use
- Field deployments
- Temporary and mobile work environments

**Who should skip it**
- Users who want the latest Wi-Fi 7 cellular platform
- Buyers who do not need battery power

---

### Spitz AX (GL-X3000) — Best Fixed Cellular Gateway

The Spitz AX is very different from the Puli AX even though both are 5G cellular routers. The Spitz AX is designed more like a fixed gateway for **homes, RVs, cabins, rural setups, and failover internet**, with **six external antennas** and a more stationary deployment style.

**Why it stands out**
- 5G NR
- Wi-Fi 6 AX3000
- Dual-SIM
- Six external antennas
- Better fit for fixed placement and signal optimization

**Best for**
- Rural internet
- RV and vehicle installs
- Backup WAN
- Users who want a stationary 5G gateway

**Who should skip it**
- Buyers who need battery power
- Users who want the smallest, most portable cellular router
